# Recommendations

Based on the analysis of self-pay enquiry conversion performance, several operational opportunities emerge that could help improve both conversion rates and overall revenue generation.

---

## 1. Strengthen Enquiry Follow-Up and Consultation Booking

The analysis shows that **36% of enquiries did not progress to consultation**, representing the largest drop-off in the patient pathway.

However, once a patient attends consultation, the likelihood of conversion increases significantly. Among patients who reached consultation, **43% progressed to surgery**.

This suggests that improving the transition from **enquiry to consultation** may have the greatest impact on overall conversion performance.

Possible actions could include:

- Improving follow-up processes for new enquiries
- Tracking enquiry status more closely
- Introducing reminder systems for consultation booking
- Reducing response times to incoming enquiries

Increasing the proportion of enquiries that reach consultation would likely improve overall surgical conversion rates.

---

## 2. Review Practices at Higher-Performing Hospital Sites

Conversion performance varies across hospital locations, with **Hospital D achieving the highest conversion rate and total revenue** in the dataset.

While this may partly reflect external factors such as location or local demand, it may also indicate differences in operational practices.

A useful next step could be to:

- Review enquiry handling and booking processes at higher-performing sites
- Identify operational practices that support stronger conversion outcomes
- Share effective approaches across other hospital locations where appropriate

Understanding these differences may help improve performance more broadly.

---

## 3. Prioritise High-Value Surgical Procedures

Revenue is strongly concentrated in **higher-value surgical procedures**, particularly:

- Knee replacement
- Hip replacement
- Gallbladder removal

These procedures generate a large proportion of total revenue despite representing a smaller share of overall cases.

Ensuring that enquiry handling, consultation availability, and patient follow-up remain strong for these procedures could help maximise revenue potential.

Possible actions could include:

- Monitoring enquiry conversion for high-value procedures
- Ensuring sufficient consultation capacity for surgical pathways
- Supporting marketing or referral activity aligned with these services

---

## 4. Monitor Consultation Waiting Times

Patients who received consultations within **two weeks of enquiry** showed higher conversion rates than the overall average.

Although differences between delay groups are relatively modest, the pattern suggests that **timely consultations may support stronger conversion outcomes**.

Possible actions could include:

- Monitoring consultation wait times for self-pay patients
- Reviewing consultation capacity where delays are longer
- Prioritising early consultation scheduling after enquiry

---

## Summary

The analysis suggests that the most meaningful opportunities to improve self-pay conversion performance are likely to be:

- Increasing the proportion of enquiries that progress to consultation
- Understanding operational differences between hospital sites
- Supporting high-value surgical procedures
- Maintaining timely consultation scheduling

Together, these actions could help improve both **conversion performance and overall revenue outcomes** within the self-pay patient pathway.
